Genetic and Medical Terms Glossary

Carcinoma
A type of cancer that starts in cells that make up the skin or the tissue lining organs, such as the mouth or throat.
Metastasis
The spread of cancer cells from the place where they first formed to another part of the body.
Biopsy
The removal of cells or tissues for examination by a pathologist to determine whether cancer is present.

DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid)
The molecule inside cells that carries genetic information and passes it from one generation to the next.
Gene Mutation
A permanent change in the DNA sequence that makes up a gene. Mutations in certain genes can increase cancer risk.
Tumor Suppressor Genes
Genes that help control cell growth. When they don't work properly, cells can grow out of control, which can lead to cancer.

Leukoplakia
White or gray patches in the mouth that can't be scraped off. These may become cancerous.
Erythroplakia
Red patches in the mouth that can't be attributed to another cause. These have a high risk of becoming cancerous.
Squamous Cell Carcinoma
The most common type of oral cancer, starting in the flat cells that cover the surfaces of the mouth, tongue, and lips.
Buccal Mucosa
The inner lining of the cheeks and lips, which can be a site for oral cancer development.

HPV (Human Papillomavirus)
A virus that can cause cell changes that may lead to cancer, including oral cancer.
Carcinogens
Substances that can cause cancer. Tobacco and alcohol are common carcinogens for oral cancer.
Genetic Predisposition
An inherited increased likelihood of developing cancer based on genetic factors.
